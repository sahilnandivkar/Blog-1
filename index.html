Start of the Journey: Mistakes/Difficulties & Solution

As every developer, I started my journey by searching for materials on YouTube and different websites. The very first mistake I made was watching videos and simultaneously replicating the code. It provided no real benefit to my learning. One day, I decided to create the frontend part of a website from scratch, and I couldn't accomplish even 50% of what I had planned. It was a moment of realization, and I decided to stop replicating codes simultaneously.


The second mistake I made was thinking that writing code on paper would help me remember it. Sounds funny, right? It was my second mistake. Writing code in a book is not a bad thing; it's an excellent way to build logic in programming (I'll talk about it in upcoming blogs, so stay tuned). However, when it comes to more extensive codes â€” for example, languages like HTML with opening and closing tags â€” it can make things a bit complex for a beginner. It might not be the case for everyone, but I faced complexity issues, and it wasted plenty of my time.


Feeling a bit disappointed, I decided to start from the very beginning. I found out that I needed to build logic for the programming language. It was like having a bag full of tools, and it was up to me to decide which tool to use for a specific requirement. This time, I watched many tutorials available on YouTube. What I did differently was to watch the entire video, note down important points, and create my own notes. Whenever I encountered errors, formatting issues, or bugs while making websites, I referred to my notes for solutions. I still have those notes, and I use them as a reference.

Conclusion: Key Learnings

I'm not an expert, and I don't have years of experience in web development. I'm still on my learning journey, discovering something new in this field each and every day. The IT field is dynamic and constantly evolving, and while I may not be able to discuss the advancements in technology like an experienced person, it's a long journey for me too. However, what I can share are the mistakes I've made as a beginner, so you can avoid similar pitfalls and save time.


Just follow these basic steps:

- Never copy-paste (at least as a beginner). It creates a bubble around you, giving the illusion that you know everything, but in reality, you'll learn nothing.


- Try to build logic; attempt to understand how things work; question why a particular thing yields a specific response. Let your mind be flooded with questions because that's how you maintain interest.


- Strive to find solutions on your own (as much as possible). If not, seek help from every possible platform without hesitating. In the end, what matters is how well you've learned that concept.


- Last but not least, don't give up. Learning programming can be frustrating at timesâ€”whether it's not finding a solution, facing a hundred bugs, or encountering any challenge. Don't give up.
